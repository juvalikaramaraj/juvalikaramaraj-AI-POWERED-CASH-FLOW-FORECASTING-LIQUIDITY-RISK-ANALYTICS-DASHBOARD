Purpose

This cash flow forecasting model analyzes monthly liquidity position and identifies potential cash surplus or deficit under Base, Best, and Worst case scenarios.

Key Findings — Base Case

  • Monthly cash flow remains positive for most periods
  • October shows negative net cash flow due to higher outflows
  • January reflects the strongest liquidity position
  • June shows a thin cash buffer and needs monitoring

Risk Analysis

  • Cash deficit risk arises if inflows slow and expenses increase
  • Worst case scenario indicates possible cash stress in September
  • Sustained negative cash flow may require external funding or cost control

Recommendations

  • Improve cash collections in low-inflow months
  • Control discretionary expenses during risky periods
  • Maintain a minimum cash buffer for operational safety
  • Regularly update assumptions for accurate forecasting
